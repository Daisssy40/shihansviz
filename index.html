About

Me

Until now

I'm Daisy (Shihan), studying IDT (CS) at the University of Hong Kong.

I've lived on my own since I was a teenager — high school in the UK, then university, then Hong Kong. That gave me something I didn't expect: a lot of time with myself, and with the people around me. I started watching how people moved through the world — what they used, how they used it, why they kept coming back. Somewhere in there I developed a habit, maybe a natural one, of reading between the lines to figure out what people actually mean. Friends call it mind-reading. To me it just feels normal. When I stumble onto a small feature in an app I hadn't noticed before, my first thought isn't 'that's useful' — it's 'how did they think of that?' That moment of connection, of guessing right, is what got me hooked.

The other thing I've known about myself for a long time: I need to build something to actually understand it. Lego first, then working through math proofs, then a long list of random skills I picked up along the way. Making isn't the point — it's how I think. Pure analysis leaves me with the feeling that something hasn't landed yet.

I have genuine admiration for people who make things that matter in other people's lives. But I also know that actually building a good product isn't about the big, audible decisions — it's about everything small that holds it together: coordination, tradeoffs, conversations that go nowhere and then suddenly do, understanding what everyone actually needs. That doesn't make it feel harder. It makes it feel more real.

I like art and exhibitions. I can stay happy for a long time over a single interaction detail that surprised me. I'm not afraid of friction — I think the conversation matters more than keeping things smooth. I learn quickly, and I have a habit of needing to explain things clearly, because if I can't explain it, I don't think I've actually learned it yet.

实验笔记

一些我在折腾的东西。

不是技能列表。是我真实做过的事情的记录——用工具解决问题，然后思考它意味着什么。

01

把 PPT 讲义转成可交互 HTML 笔记

课堂 PPT 是为「当场听」设计的，不是为「日后复习」设计的。做法是：把 PPT 导出为图片，用 Claude 逐页提取文字结构，再生成一份双栏 HTML 笔记——左侧是原始要点，右侧是补充的解释、延伸问题与关联概念。整个流程约需 15 分钟。有意思的地方不在于速度，而在于「被迫重新组织信息」这个动作本身让理解更深了。

ClaudeHTML / CSSPython
02

用 Manim 做示意动画

用 Manim 把抽象流程画成可复用的短视频脚本，让 Claude 生成场景代码，反复调整时序与视觉层级。目标不是视频本身，而是验证 AI 能否压缩「我理解了」到「我能讲清楚」之间的距离。

ManimClaudePython
03

Cursor + Claude 流水线

用 Cursor + Claude 搭建用户访谈合成脚本：转录 6 段访谈，提取 JTBD、痛点与原话，按主题归组。最有意思的部分是 AI 输出与我自己归纳的分歧——模型发现了几个我下意识忽略掉的模式。

CursorClaude APIWhisper
04

用自然语言做漏斗分析

把一份模拟产品数据集加载进 notebook，用 GPT-4o 的代码解释器通过纯自然语言提问来运行漏斗分析，不手写 SQL。有趣的发现：模型偶尔会错误地理解问题本身。而发现这一点，需要你已经知道自己在找什么。
